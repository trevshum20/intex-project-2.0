Opoid Overdose Crisis

The latest statistics indicate that the total yearly overdose deaths due to opioids reached a new high of 70,237 in 2017. Two years later, in 2019, there were still almost 50,000 people who died from opioid-involved overdoses in the United States.

Real Stories

The opioid crisis is devastating communities across America. Click here to read how this crisis is affecting real people.

The addiction to and misuse of opioids is a serious national crisis that affects not only public health but social and economic welfare as well.

Statistics


  • Roughly 21 to 29 percent of patients who are prescribed opioids for chronic pain misuse them.
  • Between 8 and 12 percent of people using an opioid for chronic pain develop an opioid use disorder.
  • Around 4 to 6 percent of people who misuse prescription opioids transition to heroin.

3 Ways to Combat the Opioid Crisis:

  • Strengthening our understanding of the epidemic through better public health surveillance.
  • Providing support for cutting-edge research on pain and addiction.
  • Advancing better practices for pain management.
